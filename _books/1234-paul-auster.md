# Introduction

“We are the product of our environment”, I had heard this sentence a while back and did not think much of it. After reading this book, I realized that this simple sentence carries more weight than I had realized. In this book, Paul Auster explores the idea of different environments and how that might affect one's life. The main character is called. Archibald, and there are four variations of his journey given different environments. In each substory, he grows up in a different city, his parents’ characters are slightly different and he develops different characteristics. These variations cause Archibald to have wildly different lives in each substory.

# Character developments

The story starts with a Russian immigrant who answers “I don’t know what you are saying” in Russian when he is asked what his name is by the immigration officer. The officer who does not know Russian puts down his name as Archibald. Our main character is the grandson of Archibald, and is named after him.

There are common characters within all the substories and it is interesting to see how the same characters interact differently with Archibald. Amy is one of those characters who Archibald will try to be with in every substory. Amy’s father and uncle are also important characters since Archibald’s mother marries each of them in two different substories. Amy is considered to be a family friend since Archibald’s mother worked for her grandfather, and that is how his mother will get to know and spend time with Amy’s family.

The characteristic variations within substories can be extremely different. For instance, in one substory his father needs money to pay for his brother’s gambling loans. So he decides to burn down his store to get money from the insurance company, but he ends up dying in the fire himself. However in another substory Archibald’s father is less compassionate toward his brother, he gets very rich and divorces Archibald’s mother. One could postulate that the contrast in father’s compassion towards others shapes Archibald's character differently, not to mention that the tragic death of his father in one substory changes his path in life dramatically.

## Environment

Environment tends to be a deciding factor in who one becomes. And the definition of environment is quite vague and somewhat vast. Environment could be loosely defined as the people and events that can influence us. Paul Auster vividly shows the effect of different environments on Archibald within the substories. In one he writes political poems, listens to music, and is heavily involved in politics of his time. Whereas in another one he is very apolitical, athletic and he is on the basketball team. Now when he is interested in politics it is easy for him to converse and attract Amy but his lack of interest in politics (in the other substory) makes it hard for him to engage with Amy, but again in the other scenario the two fall madly in love.

## People and influence

People that influence us can be anyone that we meet or don’t meet in our lifetime. It is difficult to pinpoint who has the most influence on our lives, but one could try to narrow it down at least. Most obvious ones would be the people who are there when we are growing up. This can be our parents, grandparents, and family friends. We learn so much from them. Most of our first memories will involve these individuals, and they have a great influence on us. The affection that we receive or don’t receive at a young age tends to shape our future relationships. And the interesting aspect is that we do not have a choice in who we are surrounded by when we are a child. Paul Auster portrays this beautifully, Archibald is pretty much the same character at the beginning of each substory but as he grows, starts dating, finds interests, and goes to college, the deviations within characters in each substory becomes vivid.

And for the case of people we don’t meet: Let’s imagine that a powerful country decides to invade a less powerful country and cause displacement for a large population. Some may lose the chance to attend university, get married, or simply live their lives. Now obviously they are never going to meet the leaders of the invading country but the effect can be seen in their lives. It could be argued that this type of effect happens for a large population and might be distracting from the main point. But there are extreme cases where this type of effect cannot be overlooked.

## Society-wide events

Political movements, economic recession, and depression are types of events that can affect society as a whole. It might not be obvious but these events play a major role in what is vastly defined as our environment. 

For instance, spending habits are different when the economy is flourishing than when it is collapsing. The difference in spending habits can have impacts on the way people interact with each other. They might not throw parties as much as they used to, or they might stop socializing in groups because of the cost. All these changes will alter the existing relationships as well as relationships that might have existed if the economical situation was different.

## Frame of reference

One other important aspect that encompasses both the events and people is frame of reference. This matter could be viewed from different perspectives but the Geographical Differences is an intuitive one. What do I mean by Geographical Difference? People from different parts of the world interpret the same matters differently. This causes cultural differences between nations, and this is why people from different parts of the world live life differently. More specifically this might cause cultural differences within nations as well. One of the oldest splits within societies has been liberal and conservative: where liberals try to replace old ways with new ones and conservatives tend to stand for the old way of doing things. 

For instance Some events happening in the US will be interpreted differently in the Middle East, and people’s feelings toward that event might be drastically different. And if one is living in the Middle East in that time frame where that event occured will remember it in a different way. 
This is true even within countries, for instance people feel differently about gun violence in different states of the US.
